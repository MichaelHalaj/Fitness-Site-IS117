Full Body Training Split

by Michael Halaj

What is the Full Body training split?

The full body split is a comprehensive workout routine that involves targeting all major muscle groups in a single training session. This approach offers several benefits, including increased frequency of muscle stimulation and efficient use of time in the gym. By incorporating exercises that engage multiple muscle groups simultaneously, individuals can maximize their training efforts and promote overall muscle growth and strength development. Moreover, the full body split allows for ample recovery time between sessions, as muscles are given adequate rest before being trained again.

One of the key advantages of the full body split is its effectiveness for both beginners and experienced lifters. Beginners can benefit from the simplicity and comprehensiveness of this program, as it provides a structured framework for learning basic exercises and building a foundation of strength and muscle mass. On the other hand, experienced lifters can use the full body split to maintain muscle balance, improve muscle endurance, and break through plateaus by varying intensity, volume, and exercise selection.

Another benefit of the full body split is its flexibility and scalability. Individuals can customize their workouts to suit their goals, preferences, and fitness levels by adjusting variables such as exercise order, intensity, and volume. This adaptability makes the full body split suitable for a wide range of individuals, from those focused on general fitness to competitive athletes. Additionally, the program can be modified to accommodate different training modalities, such as bodybuilding, powerlifting, or functional fitness, making it a versatile choice for individuals with diverse goals and interests.

Despite its many benefits, the full body split does have some potential drawbacks. One limitation is the need for careful planning and programming to ensure balanced muscle development and prevent overtraining. Without proper attention to exercise selection and progression, individuals may experience fatigue, injury, or stagnation in their progress. Moreover, some individuals may find it challenging to maintain intensity and focus throughout a full body workout, especially as they fatigue towards the end of the session. However, with proper planning, consistency, and attention to individual needs, the full body split can be an effective and sustainable approach to resistance training for many individuals.

Workout Split Example

Day 1: Full Body Workout

  • Squats: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Bench Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Bent Over Rows: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Shoulder Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Pull-Ups (or Assisted Pull-Ups): 3 sets x max reps

Day 2: Rest or Active Recovery

Day 3: Full Body Workout

  • Deadlifts: 3 sets x 6-8 reps
  • Dumbbell Lunges: 3 sets x 8-10 reps per leg
  • Push-Ups: 3 sets x max reps
  • Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Leg Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps

Day 4: Rest or Active Recovery

Day 5: Full Body Workout

  • Front Squats: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Incline Bench Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Barbell Rows: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Dumbbell Lateral Raises: 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Chin-Ups (or Assisted Chin-Ups): 3 sets x max reps

Day 6 & 7: Rest or Active Recovery
